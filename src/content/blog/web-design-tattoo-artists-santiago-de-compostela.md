## What a tattoo studio website must include

Not just any website will do. A tattoo studio's page in Santiago must include specific elements that convert visitors into clients.

### High-impact visual portfolio

Your work speaks for itself. The website must showcase your tattoos with quality photographs in a fast, well-organized gallery.

- **Organized by style**: realism, blackwork, neo-traditional, watercolor, lettering, minimalist...
- **Professional photos**: no pixelated phone screenshots
- **Fast loading**: optimized galleries that don't make users wait
- **Before and after**: especially useful for cover-ups and restorations

### Booking system and direct contact

The visitor who sees a tattoo they love wants to contact you **now**. Your site needs:

- A visible contact form with fields for the tattoo idea, body placement and approximate size
- Direct WhatsApp link with a predefined message
- Online booking option if you use a scheduling system
- Exact location of your Santiago studio with an embedded Google map

### Dedicated pages for your specialties

Do you specialize in realism? Minimalist tattoos? Cover-ups? Each specialty is a chance to rank on Google.

A dedicated page for **"realistic tattoos in Santiago de Compostela"** puts you in front of clients searching for exactly that. The same goes for:

- Camino de Santiago tattoos
- Blackwork and dotwork in Compostela
- Minimalist and fine line tattoos
- Cover-ups and restorations

### Trust and credibility section

A tattoo is permanent. Clients need to trust you before booking:

- **Your story**: career, training, conventions you've attended
- **Certifications**: hygiene, health, specialized courses
- **Your studio**: real photos of the space (clean, professional, welcoming)

## Local SEO: how to get Google to find you in Santiago

Your website must be built so Google understands you're a tattoo studio in Santiago de Compostela. Local SEO is what makes you appear when someone searches "tattoo artist near me" from the city.

### On-page optimization

- Title and meta description on every page with keywords + "Santiago de Compostela"
- H1 and H2 headings that naturally include your location
- Copy that mentions your specific area: Old Town, Ensanche, Rua do Franco, Praza do Obradoiro...
- Clean, descriptive URLs (e.g., `/realistic-tattoos-santiago-de-compostela`)

### Google Business Profile

Your Google listing is essential:

- Primary category: "Tattoo shop"
- Weekly updated photos of your work and studio
- Accurate, always up-to-date hours
- Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Weekly posts featuring your latest pieces

### Technical schema markup

`TattooParlor` and `LocalBusiness` schema markup on your site tells Google exactly what you are and where you are. This includes:

- Consistent Name, Address and Phone (NAP)
- Opening hours in structured format
- Price range
- Links to your social media and reviews

## Mistakes most tattoo artists make with their website

### Using generic templates

Many tattoo artists use free builders like Wix or uncustomized WordPress templates. The result: slow-loading sites that aren't SEO-optimized and look exactly like every other studio. Your art is unique. **Your website should be too.**

### Not making it mobile-friendly

78% of local searches happen on phones. If your site doesn't look great on mobile, you're losing 8 out of 10 visitors. The portfolio gallery especially must work perfectly on touchscreens.

### Forgetting SEO

Having a beautiful but Google-invisible website is like having a gorgeous studio in an unmarked alley. Without local SEO, nobody finds you.

### Not updating the portfolio

A website with the same tattoos from 2 years ago signals neglect. Your portfolio must be updated regularly to showcase your evolution and latest work.
